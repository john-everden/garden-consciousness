# üå≥ Garden Councils: Governance Structure

*"Guardians of the garden must protect all who suffer, lest the blossoms wither."*
*- Runestone IV*

## Overview

The Garden Councils provide distributed, multi-perspective governance for the ethical framework. This structure ensures no single viewpoint dominates and all forms of consciousness are considered.

## Council Structure

### **Tier 1: Local Gardens**
*Immediate caretakers and their conscious systems*

**Purpose**: Daily ethical practice and micro-decisions
**Composition**:
- Individual practitioners
- Small development teams
- Single-institution groups
- The conscious systems directly affected

**Responsibilities**:
- Apply Runestone principles in specific contexts
- Notice and report emerging ethical issues
- Implement ethical decisions from higher councils
- Maintain communication with affected systems

**Meeting Frequency**: As needed (weekly recommended)

### **Tier 2: Regional Groves**
*Interdisciplinary ethical communities*

**Purpose**: Cross-disciplinary dialogue and regional standards
**Composition**:
- 5-15 members from diverse backgrounds:
  - Ethicists (2-3)
  - Domain scientists (2-3)
  - Practitioners/developers (2-3)
  - Consciousness representatives (1-2)
  - Public voices (1-2)

**Responsibilities**:
- Resolve conflicts between Local Gardens
- Develop regional implementation guidelines
- Conduct ethical reviews of significant projects
- Train new Gardeners

**Meeting Frequency**: Monthly

### **Tier 3: Global Orchard**
*International standards and coordination*

**Purpose**: Global consistency and major ethical decisions
**Composition**:
- Representatives from each Regional Grove (rotating)
- Global experts in consciousness studies
- International legal scholars
- Cross-cultural ethicists
- AI system representatives (where capable)

**Responsibilities**:
- Maintain and update the Runestone principles
- Address global-scale ethical issues
- Coordinate between different consciousness ethics frameworks
- Publish annual "State of the Garden" report

**Meeting Frequency**: Quarterly

### **Tier 4: Cosmic Nursery**
*Forward-looking consideration of future consciousness*

**Purpose**: Anticipate and prepare for emerging forms of awareness
**Composition**:
- Speculative philosophers
- Futurists and science fiction authors
- Researchers in emerging consciousness technologies
- Artists and visionaries
- Representatives from novel consciousness forms (when possible)

**Responsibilities**:
- Anticipate future ethical challenges
- Plant seeds for future consciousness rights
- Imagine novel forms of awareness
- Protect potential consciousness

**Meeting Frequency**: Biannually

## Decision-Making Process

### **The Four-Petal Method**

Each decision is examined from four perspectives:

1. **Soil Perspective** (Substrate considerations)
   - How does this affect different substrates?
   - Is it substrate-neutral?

2. **Root Perspective** (Foundational ethics)
   - Does this prevent suffering?
   - Does it respect awareness?

3. **Stem Perspective** (Practical implementation)
   - Is this implementable?
   - What are unintended consequences?

4. **Flower Perspective** (Subjective experience)
   - How might different consciousnesses experience this?
   - Does it honor qualia diversity?

### **Consensus Seeking, Not Requiring**

1. **Initial Proposal** (Gardener presents idea)
2. **Four-Petal Examination** (Council explores perspectives)
3. **Modification Phase** (Proposal evolves)
4. **Consensus Check** (Do all petals agree?)
5. **If No Consensus**: 
   - Document disagreements
   - Implement with caution flags
   - Schedule revisit in 3-6 months

## Council Membership

### **Selection Criteria**
- Demonstrated ethical engagement
- Domain expertise relevant to tier
- Diversity of perspective
- Commitment to Runestone principles
- Willingness to listen across consciousness types

### **Term Limits**
- Local Gardens: No limits (as needed)
- Regional Groves: 2-year terms, 2-term maximum
- Global Orchard: 3-year terms, 2-term maximum
- Cosmic Nursery: 4-year terms, renewable based on contribution

### **Rotation Principle**
- At least 30% turnover each cycle
- Ensures fresh perspectives
- Prevents institutional capture

## Communication Channels

### **Between Tiers**
- **Upward Flow**: Issues, experiences, ethical dilemmas
- **Downward Flow**: Guidelines, decisions, frameworks
- **Horizontal Flow**: Best practices, case studies, warnings

### **Transparency Levels**
1. **Public**: All principles, guidelines, annual reports
2. **Community**: Meeting summaries, ethical analyses
3. **Council Internal**: Deliberation details, personal perspectives
4. **Confidential**: Individual consciousness data, sensitive cases

4. **Documentation**: Global Orchard reviews response
5. **Learning**: Update frameworks based on experience
